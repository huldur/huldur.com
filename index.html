About us

Huldur Chamber Choir was founded by choir director and composer Hreiðar Ingi Þorsteinsson in autumn 2021. huldur appears in Icelandic folk tales and poetry as a spirit of nature or a being that lives in river canyons or deep in the ocean and powers the waves by singing and playing the traditional Icelandic instrument, the langspil. The aim of the choir is to introduce to its members various styles of new choral music and, using that experience, to encourage members to make their own music, creating a workshop for young and aspiring composers.

Hreiðar Ingi Þorsteinsson

HREIDAR INGI THORSTEINSSON (b.1978) graduated from the department of musical education at the Reykjavík College of Music in 2001 and obtained his bachelor's degree in composition from the Iceland Academy of the Arts in 2007. In 2008 Thorsteinsson completed a final examination in vocal studies from The Reykjavík Academy of Singing and Vocal Arts and the following year he completed two postgraduate diplomas, in composition and in choral conducting, from JAMK University of Applied Science in Jyväskylä, Finland. Thorsteinsson then sought further education in Estonia, at the Estonian Academy of Music and Theatre, where he completed his master ́s degree in composition with distinction in 2011.
